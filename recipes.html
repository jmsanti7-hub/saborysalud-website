Recipes

These recipes are designed to feel familiar, simple, and realistic. They focus on protein, fiber, and flavor — no complicated ingredients or extreme rules.

1. High-Protein Breakfast Bowl

A quick balanced breakfast you can throw together before work or class.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup cooked oats or cooked quinoa
  • 3/4 cup Greek yogurt (or dairy-free yogurt)
  • 1 scoop protein powder (optional)
  • 1/2 cup mixed berries (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 tablespoon nut butter (almond, peanut, or sunflower)
  • 1–2 teaspoons honey or maple syrup (optional)

Directions

  1. Place the cooked oats or quinoa in a bowl.
  2. Stir the protein powder into the yogurt until smooth.
  3. Top the grains with the yogurt mixture.
  4. Add berries on top and drizzle with nut butter.
  5. Sweeten with honey or maple syrup if desired and enjoy.

2. Sabor y Salud Chicken & Rice Bowl

A simplified, weeknight-friendly version of a comforting arroz-style bowl.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked brown or white rice
  • 4 oz cooked chicken breast or chicken thigh, chopped
  • 1/2 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup sautéed peppers and onions
  • 1/4 cup corn (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1–2 tablespoons salsa or pico de gallo
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or avocado oil
  • Salt, pepper, garlic powder, and cumin to taste
  • Fresh lime and cilantro (optional)

Directions

  1. Heat a pan over medium heat and add the oil.
  2. Add peppers and onions and cook until softened.
  3. Stir in the chicken, black beans, corn, and seasonings.
  4. Warm everything through, stirring occasionally.
  5. Place the rice in a bowl and top with the chicken mixture.
  6. Finish with salsa, a squeeze of lime, and cilantro if desired.

3. Café con Protein Smoothie

A coffee-based smoothie for busy mornings when you want caffeine, protein, and something cold and easy.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup cooled brewed coffee
  • 1/2 cup milk or milk alternative
  • 1 scoop vanilla or chocolate protein powder
  • 1 small frozen banana (or 1/2 for less sweetness)
  • 1 tablespoon peanut butter or almond butter
  • 3–5 ice cubes (optional, for a thicker texture)

Directions

  1. Add coffee, milk, protein powder, banana, and nut butter to a blender.
  2. Blend until smooth. Add ice if you want it thicker and blend again.
  3. Taste and adjust sweetness if needed.
  4. Pour into a glass and enjoy as a breakfast or snack.
